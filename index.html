Why these matter for ES / NQ
IndicatorImpactWhat it means for futures
CPI / Core PCE
 High
Hot print → rates stay high → ES/NQ sell-off. Cool print → rally fuel. Biggest single-day vol driver.
Fed funds rate
 High
Sets the risk-free rate. Rising rate regime compresses PE multiples — especially damaging for NQ (tech heavy).
10-yr / 2-yr yields
 High
10-yr drives equity discount rates. 2s10s inversion historically precedes recession — uninversion = regime shift signal.
NFP / Jobless claims
 High
Strong jobs = Fed stays hawkish. Weak jobs = rate cut narrative. Claims give a weekly early read on labor softening.
GDP (advance)
 Med
Advance print moves markets most. Confirms or denies recession narrative already priced in by markets.
ISM PMI
 Med
Below 50 = contraction. Forward-looking. New orders sub-index most market-moving. Often leads GDP by months.
Retail sales
 Med
Consumer spending = ~70% of GDP. A miss feeds the recession/stagflation trade and can push ES toward key support.
Consumer sentiment
 Low
Lagging indicator. Best for confirming consumer stress themes over weeks, not for intraday trading setups.

All times Pacific (PT). NY open = 6:30 AM PT. The major macro prints hit at 5:30 AM PT — be at your desk or flat before these drop.

Monday Lightest day — survey & sentiment data
7:00 AM PT
ISM Manufacturing PMI Med
First Mon of month. New orders sub-index is most critical. Sub-50 = contraction signal for ES.
7:00 AM PT
ISM Services PMI Med
First Mon of month (services week). Prices paid sub-index = inflation pressure read.
Varies
Fed speaker remarks Med
Check Fed calendar. Hawkish tone = yields up, futures down. Watch for dissent vs consensus language.
Tuesday Jobs & confidence data
5:30 AM PT
JOLTS job openings Med
~1st Tue of month. Fed watches quits rate as wage pressure proxy. Pre-market — futures already reacting at this hour.
7:00 AM PT
Consumer confidence (Conference Board) Low
Last Tue of month. Divergence from UMich can signal a consumer stress shift.
Varies
Treasury auctions (2-yr, 5-yr) Low
Weak auction = yields spike = equity headwind. Bid-to-cover ratio is the key tell.
Wednesday Highest vol day — CPI & FOMC
5:30 AM PT
CPI High
~12th of each month. Biggest single-day vol driver for ES/NQ. Core MoM surprises = 1–2% gap at open. Be positioned or flat before this.
7:30 AM PT
EIA crude oil inventories Low
Weekly. Primarily a CL driver, but energy prices feed the broader CPI/inflation narrative.
11:00 AM PT
FOMC rate decision + statement High
8x per year. Initial spike on decision, then second vol wave on Powell presser at 11:30 AM PT. Avoid entering new positions into the print.
11:30 AM PT
Powell press conference High
Quarterly (dot plot meetings). Every word parsed for forward guidance. "Data dependent" = wider ranges through close.
Varies
Treasury 10-yr auction Med
Mid-month. Weak demand = yields jump = NQ pressure into late session.
Thursday Weekly jobs + PPI — busy pre-market
5:30 AM PT
Initial jobless claims High
Every Thursday without fail. Most frequent labor read. Spiking claims = labor softening = rate cut narrative accelerates.
5:30 AM PT
PPI (producer prices) Med
~11th of month. Hot PPI = leading CPI signal. Core PPI services = most Fed-relevant sub-index.
5:30 AM PT
Retail sales Med
~15th of month. Beat = strong consumer = less cut urgency. Miss = growth scare into the open.
5:30 AM PT
GDP advance / revision High
Quarterly (advance ~last Thu of Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct). Advance print moves most. Negative print = recession confirmation.
Friday Biggest vol potential — NFP + PCE
5:30 AM PT
Non-farm payrolls (NFP) High
First Fri of month. The most anticipated release. Miss = Fed pivot narrative. Beat = hawkish fear. Avg hourly earnings equally important. Futures gap at open.
5:30 AM PT
PCE price index High
Last Fri of month. Fed's preferred inflation gauge. Target = core PCE MoM at 0.17% or below.
5:30 AM PT
Personal income & spending Low
Released with PCE. Spending trend confirms or contradicts retail sales direction.
7:00 AM PT
UMich consumer sentiment (final) Low
Last Fri of month. 5-yr inflation expectations sub-index is the key watch — a move here gets Fed attention immediately.

Times in Pacific (PT). PDT = UTC−7 (Mar–Nov) · PST = UTC−8 (Nov–Mar).
High = expect 0.5–2%+ ES/NQ move on release · Med = 0.25–0.75% directional · Low = context/confirmation only.
